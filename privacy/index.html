Auditex
Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 23, 2026

Auditex keeps tenant evidence local by default. The product is operated from the user machine and does not host, relay, or store customer tenant evidence on Magrathean infrastructure unless an operator deliberately exports material.

Data Controller

MAGRATHEAN UK LTD., a company registered in England and Wales (Company No. 16955343) with registered office at 16 Caledonian Court West Street, Watford, England, WD17 1RY, is the controller of any personal data processed in connection with Auditex. Questions and data-rights requests should be sent to contact@magrathean.uk.

What Auditex Reads

Auditex is designed for authorised Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace tenant audit work. It reads configuration, posture, metadata, permission, and audit evidence needed to generate reports and blockers. Public Auditex v1 is audit-only and does not perform remediation automation.

Local Processing and Storage

Raw evidence, normalized records, reports, manifests, blockers, and customer handoff packs are written to the local output directory selected by the operator. Auditex does not upload this evidence to MAGRATHEAN UK LTD. servers.

No Content-Read Policy

Auditex aims to avoid reading Gmail bodies, Drive file content, Calendar event content, Exchange mailbox bodies, SharePoint file content, OneDrive file content, documents, or other end-user content. Where provider scopes are broad, Auditex records scope risk and API usage so an operator can review what was attempted.

Credentials

Authentication state, saved CLI contexts, tokens, and provider credentials remain in the local operator environment or platform credential stores configured by the operator. You are responsible for securing the machine and accounts used to run Auditex.

Legal Basis

Where limited personal data is processed locally in order to run an authorised audit, processing is based on legitimate interests under Article 6(1)(f) of the UK GDPR and, where applicable, the tenant owner or operator's own lawful basis for the audit.

Retention

Because Auditex writes output locally, retention is controlled by the operator. Remove output directories, credential state, and exported packs when they are no longer needed for the authorised audit purpose.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, you may request access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, portability where applicable, and withdrawal of consent where processing is based on consent. Contact us at contact@magrathean.uk.

Complaints

You may complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint. We would appreciate the opportunity to address concerns directly first.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The Last updated date shows when the current version took effect.
