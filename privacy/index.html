Auditex
Privacy Policy

Last updated: 25 May 2026

Auditex is a Python-first CLI and MCP toolkit for Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace tenant audits. It is designed to keep raw evidence local by default.

Auditex runs in the operator environment. It does not host, relay, or store customer tenant evidence on Magrathean infrastructure unless an authorised operator deliberately sends material to us for support or a separate written agreement says otherwise.

Controller and roles

MAGRATHEAN UK LTD., a company registered in England and Wales (Company No. 16955343) with registered office at 16 Caledonian Court, West Street, Watford, England, WD17 1RY is the controller for personal data we process for our website, access requests, licensing, support, security, business administration, and customer communications.

For tenant evidence collected by Auditex in your environment, the tenant owner, customer, employer, or authorised operator is normally the controller. Magrathean is not a processor for tenant evidence unless a separate written processing agreement says so.

Operators are responsible for making sure their tenant-audit processing has a lawful basis, proper authorisation, appropriate notices, and appropriate customer or internal approvals.

What Auditex reads

Auditex may read Microsoft 365, Entra ID, Exchange, SharePoint, OneDrive, Intune, Defender, DNS, Google Workspace, Directory, Reports, Alert Center, Gmail settings, Drive metadata, Groups settings, Calendar sharing, device, OAuth grant, domain, audit-log, API-call, and security-control metadata needed to produce evidence, blockers, reports, proof tables, setup guidance, and customer packs.

This data may include personal data such as:

  • names and email addresses;
  • group, role, licence, device, and admin assignments;
  • sign-in, audit-event, alert, mailbox-setting, sharing, and permission metadata;
  • OAuth grant, app consent, API inventory, secure-score, device, and domain posture metadata;
  • customer environment names, tenant identifiers, domain names, and operational notes.

No content-read policy

Auditex public audit collectors must not read Gmail message bodies, Google Drive file content, Exchange mailbox body content, SharePoint file content, or OneDrive file content.

Some provider scopes may have broad or write-capable names even when used for read-only posture checks. Auditex records scope risk, observed API usage, read/write classification, and content-read status so the operator can review what was attempted and what was blocked.

Local processing and storage

Auditex writes audit outputs to the local output directory selected by the operator. Outputs may include:

  • raw evidence;
  • normalized records;
  • API inventory;
  • data-handling declarations;
  • audit plans;
  • blockers and diagnostics;
  • validation reports;
  • report packs;
  • customer handoff packs;
  • checksums;
  • evidence indexes;
  • proof tables;
  • audit logs;
  • MCP and AI-safe context files.

Auditex does not upload these bundles to Magrathean servers by default. Treat every run directory and customer pack as confidential.

Credentials and authentication material

Azure CLI sessions, OAuth tokens, refresh material, Google service-account files, OAuth client files, OAuth cache files, app credentials, bearer material, signing keys, and other secrets remain in the local operator environment or configured platform credential stores.

Do not send those materials to Magrathean, commit them to source control, place them in customer packs, paste them into AI tools, or leave them in shared logs.

MCP and AI use

Auditex MCP and AI-safe context surfaces are intended to reason from selected bundle artifacts such as summaries, data-handling declarations, audit plans, API inventory, report packs, proof tables, validation, and AI-safe context files.

Do not send raw credentials, secrets, raw tenant exports, or unnecessary personal data to third-party AI services. Operators are responsible for checking customer and internal rules before using AI with Auditex outputs.

Website, access requests, licensing, and support

If you email us, request access, report a security issue, send support material, or enter into a commercial arrangement, we process the contact details and content you provide so we can respond, administer the relationship, protect the product, and keep records.

Do not send tenant evidence, secrets, regulated data, or customer data unless we have agreed a secure support route in writing.

  • legitimate interests to operate, secure, improve, support, document, defend, and commercialise Auditex;
  • consent where required for optional communications;

Operators remain responsible for their own lawful basis for tenant-audit processing.

We may use service providers for email, hosting, security, licensing, legal, accounting, support, and operational administration. Where a provider processes personal data for us, we use appropriate contractual controls.

Tenant evidence is shared with us only if you deliberately send it or if a separate written agreement provides a supported processing route.

Retention

Auditex output retention is controlled by the operator because the output is local. Delete run directories, auth caches, exported reports, and customer packs when they are no longer needed for the authorised audit purpose.

We keep contact, support, security, licensing, and business records only for as long as needed for the purposes described in this policy, legal compliance, dispute handling, and auditability.

For controller records we hold, retention depends on the type of record and the risk involved. Support and customer communications are kept only while needed to answer the request, maintain the relationship, handle disputes, or preserve auditability. Security records are kept for investigation, defence, and abuse-prevention periods. Accounting, tax, company, contract, licensing, and business records are kept for the period required by law or for ordinary limitation periods, normally up to six years where relevant. We delete or anonymise records when they are no longer needed.

Security

Auditex is designed around local evidence handling, no default external analytics, no default tenant-evidence upload, read-only audit contracts, API inventory, customer-pack verification, and no-content-read validation.

No method of storage or transmission is completely secure. Operators must protect the machines, credentials, output folders, transfer channels, AI tools, customer-pack recipients, and retention locations used for each run.
