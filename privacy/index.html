Auditex privacy policy

Last updated: 26 May 2026

Auditex is designed to keep audit evidence local by default. Website and support contact data are processed separately from operator-side tenant evidence.

Controller and roles

MAGRATHEAN UK LTD. is the controller for website, access-request, licensing, support, and business-contact processing. Tenant evidence collected by Auditex in your own environment is usually controlled by the tenant owner or authorised operator, not by Magrathean.

What Auditex reads

Auditex may read tenant metadata needed for audit evidence, such as directory, sharing, settings, posture, device, alert, audit-log, and API-usage data. That data can still contain personal data such as names, email addresses, role assignments, group membership, and operational notes.

No content-read policy

Public Auditex audit collectors must not read Gmail message bodies, Drive file content, Exchange mailbox body content, SharePoint file content, or OneDrive file content.

Local processing and storage

Auditex writes outputs to the local directory chosen by the operator. Outputs may include raw evidence, normalized records, API inventory, proof tables, validation reports, report packs, handoff packs, and checksums.

Auditex does not upload those bundles to Magrathean servers by default. Treat every run directory as confidential.

MCP and AI use

Auditex MCP and AI-safe context surfaces are intended to reason from selected bundle artifacts. Operators are responsible for deciding whether their customer, employer, and internal rules permit AI use with any saved output.

Retention and security

Operators control retention for local Auditex outputs. Website, support, and business records are retained only as long as needed for the stated purpose, legal compliance, and auditability.

No method of storage or transmission is completely secure. Operators must protect their machines, output folders, transfer paths, and review tools.

Your rights

Under UK data-protection law, you may have rights to access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection, portability where applicable, and withdrawal of consent where processing is based on consent.
